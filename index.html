5.1 — Trig Ratios of Acute Angles SOHCAHTOA + Reciprocals
📐
Labelling sides: Put your finger on the angle you're working with. The adjacent side touches it (says "AHA" next to your finger). The opposite is across. The hypotenuse is always opposite the right angle — the longest side.
Primary Ratios (SOH CAH TOA) sin θ = opp/hyp    cos θ = adj/hyp    tan θ = opp/adj
Reciprocal Ratios csc θ = hyp/opp = 1/sin θ sec θ = hyp/adj = 1/cos θ cot θ = adj/opp = 1/tan θ
⚠️
KEY EXAM FACT: sin and cos are ALWAYS ≤ 1 for acute angles (you divide by the largest side, the hypotenuse). Therefore csc and sec are ALWAYS ≥ 1. Tan can be anything.
🔢
Calculator trick for reciprocals: csc(25°) = 1 ÷ sin(25°). You press: 1 ÷ sin(25) =. There is NO csc button.
🔄
Finding angle from reciprocal ratio: cot θ = 1.2711 → tan θ = 1/1.2711 = 0.7867 → θ = tan⁻¹(0.7867) ≈ 38°

45-45-90 Triangle: Two equal legs of 1 → hypotenuse = √2. Angles: 45°, 45°, 90°
🔺
30-60-90 Triangle: Start with equilateral side = 2, cut in half. Short leg = 1, hypotenuse = 2, long leg = √3. Angles: 30°, 60°, 90°

💡
Pattern trick: sin 30° = cos 60° = 1/2. sin 60° = cos 30° = √3/2. They swap! Notice sin increases and cos decreases as angle goes 30→60.
🎯
"Exact value" on test = use special triangles. No decimals allowed! When you see the word "exact", your calculator stays in your pocket.
Example: (sin 45°)(cos 45°) + (sin 30°)(sin 60°) = (√2/2)(√2/2) + (1/2)(√3/2) = 2/4 + √3/4 = (2 + √3)/4
5.3 — Trig Ratios for Angles > 90° CAST Rule
📍
Standard Position: Vertex at origin, initial arm on positive x-axis, terminal arm rotates counterclockwise (positive angle) or clockwise (negative angle).
🔄
Related Acute Angle (β): The SHORTEST angle from the x-axis to the terminal arm. Always measured to the nearest x-axis — like bird wings flapping up or down from the x-axis.
📐
Principal Angle (θ): The full counterclockwise angle from 0° to 360°.

Using a point P(x, y) with radius r: sin = y/r, cos = x/r, tan = y/x

Only Sine +
Q2: 90°–180°

ALL positive
Q1: 0°–90°

Only Tan +
Q3: 180°–270°

Only Cos +
Q4: 270°–360°

Read counterclockwise starting Q1: A → S → T → C | "All Students Take Calculus"

🔢
Finding related acute angle:
Q2: β = 180° − θ   |   Q3: β = θ − 180°   |   Q4: β = 360° − θ
Key Relationships (θ is the related acute angle) Q2: sin(180°−θ) = sinθ, cos(180°−θ) = −cosθ, tan(180°−θ) = −tanθ Q3: sin(180°+θ) = −sinθ, cos(180°+θ) = −cosθ, tan(180°+θ) = tanθ Q4: sin(360°−θ) = −sinθ, cos(360°−θ) = cosθ, tan(360°−θ) = −tanθ
5.4 — Trig Ratios: Any Angle 0°–360° Two Solutions
📊
Axis angles: sin 90° = 1, cos 90° = 0, tan 90° = undefined | sin 180° = 0, cos 180° = −1 | sin 270° = −1, cos 270° = 0 | sin 360° = 0, cos 360° = 1
🎯
Two solutions always exist (between 0°–360°) for sin, cos, and tan — one in each quadrant where the ratio is positive or negative.
Step-by-step: Find all θ where sin θ = 0.4815, 0° ≤ θ ≤ 360° 1. β = sin⁻¹(0.4815) ≈ 28.8° (related acute angle) 2. sin is + in Q1 and Q2 (CAST) 3. θ = 28.8° (Q1) and θ = 180° − 28.8° = 151.2° (Q2) ✓
From a point P(x, y): find R, then ratios r = √(x² + y²) → sin θ = y/r, cos θ = x/r, tan θ = y/x Principal angle: use tan⁻¹(|y/x|) to get β, then adjust for quadrant
⚠️
Quadrant adjustment for principal angle:
Q1: θ = β | Q2: θ = 180° − β | Q3: θ = 180° + β | Q4: θ = 360° − β
5.5 — Trigonometric Identities Prove Both Sides
📋
All identities you MUST know:
Reciprocal Identities cscθ = 1/sinθ   secθ = 1/cosθ   cotθ = 1/tanθ
Quotient Identities tanθ = sinθ/cosθ    cotθ = cosθ/sinθ
Pythagorean Identities (THE BIG THREE) sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ 1 + cot²θ = csc²θ Rearrangements: sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ cos²θ = 1 − sin²θ

Strategy for proving identities:

1
Work on the MORE complex side only
2
Convert everything to sin and cos (break down tan, csc, sec, cot)
3
Factor or find common denominator to combine fractions
4
Look for sin²+cos²=1 to replace with 1 (or reverse)
5
Difference of squares: a²−b² = (a+b)(a−b) — recognise and use!
Worked example: Prove tan φ = (sin φ + sin²φ) / (cos φ)(1 + sin φ) RHS = sinφ(1 + sinφ) / cosφ(1 + sinφ) [factor sinφ from numerator] = sinφ / cosφ = tanφ = LHS ✓
5.6 — The Sine Law & Ambiguous Case SSA Warning!
Sine Law — use when you have a matching angle-side pair a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC OR equivalently: sinA/a = sinB/b = sinC/c
🔍
When to use: You have an angle-opposite-side pair (X pattern). NOT a right triangle. Given: angle-angle-side (AAS) or angle-side-side (SSA — check ambiguous case!)

Ambiguous Case (SSA — given ∠A, side a, side b):

Height h = b·sin A. This is the minimum side length to form a triangle.

No Triangle
a < h = b·sinA
0 solutions
Side too short to reach baseline
Right Triangle
a = h = b·sinA
1 solution (90°)
Exactly touches baseline
⚠️ TWO Triangles
h < a < b
2 solutions!
∠B' = 180° − ∠B (the obtuse solution)
One Triangle
a ≥ b
1 solution
Long enough, only one way
💡
Obtuse angle in ambiguous case: If sinB gives angle B, the second solution is B₂ = 180° − B. Check: does A + B₂ < 180°? If yes, triangle 2 is valid.
5.7 — The Cosine Law SAS or SSS
Cosine Law (finding a side — SAS) a² = b² + c² − 2bc·cosA b² = a² + c² − 2ac·cosB c² = a² + b² − 2ab·cosC
Cosine Law (finding an angle — SSS) cosA = (b² + c² − a²) / (2bc) cosB = (a² + c² − b²) / (2ac) cosC = (a² + b² − c²) / (2ab)
⚠️
BIGGEST MISTAKE: When using a² = b² + c² − 2bc·cosA, you must subtract the ENTIRE term 2bc·cosA. Do it all at once in the calculator: enter b²+c²−2bc·cos(A) all at once, then take √.
📋
Decision tree: Right triangle? → SOHCAHTOA | Has matching pair? → Sine Law | SAS or SSS? → Cosine Law
💡
Note: If angle = 90°, cosine law reduces to Pythagorean theorem (the −2bc·cos90° = 0 term vanishes).
5.8 — 3D Trig Problems with Bearings Sketch First!
🧭
Bearing: Clockwise angle measured from due North. Bearing 070° = 70° clockwise from North. Bearing 345° = almost all the way around (only 15° left of North).
📐
Angle of depression: The angle measured downward from a horizontal line of sight to an object below. By alternate interior angles (Z-pattern with parallel lines), the angle of depression equals the angle of elevation at the base.
3D Problem Strategy (e.g. cliff/helicopter above 2 objects) Step 1: Subtract bearings → angle between the two objects at the base Step 2: Use tan(angle of depression) = height/horizontal distance → distance to each object = height / tan(angle) Step 3: Now have SAS → use Cosine Law to find distance between objects
🔺
Z-pattern (Alternate Interior Angles): North lines are parallel. When a line cuts two parallel lines, alternate interior angles are equal. This lets you transfer a bearing angle from one point to another.
Example: Cliff 9m high, bearings 250° and 145°, depression 52° and 48° Angle between = 250 − 145 = 105° dist₁ = 9/tan(52°) ≈ 7.03 m dist₂ = 9/tan(48°) ≈ 8.10 m d² = 7.03² + 8.10² − 2(7.03)(8.10)cos(105°) d ≈ 12.02 m

⚠️ Common Mistakes to AVOID
❌
Sine law: Forgetting to check for the ambiguous case when given SSA. You MUST calculate h = b·sinA and compare.
❌
Cosine law: Stopping after b²+c² and forgetting to subtract 2bc·cosA. The ENTIRE term is subtracted.
❌
Identities: Working on BOTH sides simultaneously. Always work on ONE side only.
❌
CAST: Forgetting there are ALWAYS 2 solutions between 0°–360° for sin, cos, tan equations.
❌
Exact values: Using a calculator when question says "exact." Use special triangles only!
❌
Reciprocals: Mixing up which is the reciprocal of which. Remember: sine → cosecant, cosine → secant (they switch!)
❌
3D: Forgetting that the right angles are at the base (ground level), not in the tilted view.
